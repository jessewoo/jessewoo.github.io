Case Study: RCSB PDB Frontend - Sierra

in-depth look at modularizing our monolithic web application


Reasons and Beginnings

Our Classic Site was a Mess

Built with outdated technology and years of different teams of developers working on the code base, it became clear over the years that it would be difficult to maintain and manage. There were bandages on top of bandages, a lot of dead or redundant code, copy and pasted code littered everywhere, and a mixture of frontend and backend code mashed into one monolithic code base where the separation of development task was difficult to assign, something had to change!

Even starting up the JAVA instances took more than 5 minutes - locally - which was a pain point for developers, such inefficient usage of time. There were too many dependencies that wasn't managed well, failed pages that didn't fail safe, too many stack traces that didn't make sense and in general, the team was working with code dated back 10 years prior, which was opapue and hard to troubleshoot.

Sorry for ranting, but this was our problem that we wanted to solve for future success.

Gathering Inspiration and Going to the Drawing Board

Reading up on various tech articles, many well known companies like Netflix and Amazon moved their monolithic site to a microservice based architecture, which really inspired us to think similar lines. The advantage was speed, scale and agility, three things that were important to us for the future. In addition, there was Walmart who started using NodeJS for end to end usage of JS, which was inspiration from the best.

So taking from top notch companies, we wanted to move our code base in the same direction. As a team, RCSB PDB would start fresh and move to a new microservice-based architecture to better scale our services to accommodate growth and user demand, and to increase the speed at which we can deploy new services. And there were so many benefits even from the user. In parallel, we are streamlining our URLs for easy access and sharing.
